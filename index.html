Interest in IT

Honestly, I’m not too sure what my interest in IT is, but I do find it very intriguing. What I mean by intriguing is that I am honestly very amazed at how fast our technology is improving throughout the years, despite the internet being invented less than 30 years ago in 1989. My interest in IT started when I was in primary school when I would watch my auntie’s ex-boyfriend fix my mother’s old Pentium 4 computer. Her computer would constantly come up with a boot up error code and while he spent all day fixing it, I would just sit there and watch him. I don’t have many IT experiences to look back at but building custom computers would be one that interests me. The reason why I chose to come to RMIT was because I have heard great things about the university and that it offers a lot of IT Courses. RMIT has great reputation in the IT industry compared to Monash, Melbourne, Deakin and Swinburne University. I read on the website that RMIT has partnerships with Google, IBM, Deloitte and ANZ so that would help in the process of finding a job after I have finished my course. What I expect to learn during my studies is that I gain life skills during the process throughout the course. Life skills such as focus, critical thinking, improving my communication skills and the ability to take on any challenge that is thrown to me. I heard that from friends that have studied this course have said assignments would give me plenty of headaches from all the error codes from coding and all the stress. After completing this course, I hope by then I will be able to code with ease as coding is one of my weak points. I have discovered I enjoy subjects such as databasing or even networking.
